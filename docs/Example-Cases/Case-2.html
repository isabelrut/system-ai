Digital Product Passport (DPP) Requirements for a Large Retailer in the Toys Sector with High Compliance Ambition (Competitive Advantage)

ID: R-001
Statement: When acting as a retailer in the toys sector, the organization shall require all incoming toys to be accompanied by a complete and verified Digital Product Passport (DPP) containing mandatory product data.
Rationale: To ensure traceability, sustainability, and compliance with EU product policy instruments, and to leverage DPP data as a competitive advantage in marketing and supply chain transparency.
Source: Draft Preparatory Study on Textiles for Product Policy Instruments (working_group)
Priority: High
Risk: Medium: Relies on supplier cooperation and data availability.
Verification Criterion: 100% of incoming toys are accompanied by DPPs that meet the mandatory data criteria, verified quarterly via internal audits.

ID: R-002
Statement: The organization shall integrate DPP data into its internal systems to provide real-time consumer-facing product sustainability information via the company’s e-commerce platform and in-store displays.
Rationale: To enhance brand reputation and support informed consumer choices, leveraging DPP data as a competitive differentiator.
Source: Ideas for reducing the reporting burden using the DPP (working_group)
Priority: High
Risk: Medium: Requires integration effort and may depend on third-party APIs.
Verification Criterion: DPP data is displayed on 100% of relevant product pages and in-store kiosks within 6 months of implementation.

ID: R-003
Statement: The organization shall establish a DPP management team to oversee the collection, verification, and use of DPP data across the supply chain.
Rationale: To maintain data quality, improve supply chain collaboration, and ensure consistent compliance and competitive advantage.
Source: D4.1 Reference Architecture (working_group)
Priority: High
Risk: Low: Internal process change with moderate resource allocation.
Verification Criterion: A dedicated team is trained and operational within 3 months, with monthly performance reviews.

ID: R-004
Statement: The organization shall implement a system to audit the authenticity and accuracy of DPPs received from suppliers using trusted identifiers and verified data sources.
Rationale: To ensure trustworthiness of DPP data and prevent fraud or misrepresentation.
Source: D4.1 Reference Architecture (working_group)
Priority: High
Risk: Medium: Requires integration with external verification systems.
Verification Criterion: 100% of incoming DPPs are audited for authenticity using verified identities and data sources within 6 months.

ID: R-005
Statement: The organization shall use DPP data to generate reports for internal stakeholders and external bodies, including environmental impact assessments and supply chain sustainability metrics.
Rationale: To support internal decision-making and public transparency, enhancing the organization’s reputation and compliance posture.
Source: Ideas for reducing the reporting burden using the DPP (working_group)
Priority: Medium
Risk: Low: Reporting can be automated with existing systems.
Verification Criterion: Reports are generated quarterly and reviewed by senior management with feedback documented.

ID: R-006
Statement: The organization shall collaborate with upstream suppliers to improve DPP data completeness and accuracy, offering training and support where necessary.
Rationale: To strengthen the entire supply chain’s compliance and data quality, aligning with competitive advantage goals.
Source: Draft Preparatory Study on Textiles for Product Policy Instruments (working_group)
Priority: Medium
Risk: Medium: Depends on supplier willingness and capability.
Verification Criterion: At least 80% of top-tier suppliers participate in training programs and improve DPP data quality within 9 months.

ID: R-007
Statement: The organization shall align its DPP data models with high-level concepts and aligned ontologies to support harmonized data queries and interoperability.
Rationale: To facilitate data exchange with other stakeholders and reduce the need for manual data handling.
Source: D4.1 Reference Architecture (working_group)
Priority: Medium
Risk: Medium: Requires technical coordination and alignment.
Verification Criterion: Data models are aligned with industry ontologies and used in at least two interoperability tests within 12 months.

ID: R-008
Statement: The organization shall use the DPP as a foundation for broader reporting obligations, reducing redundant data collection and improving data reuse across regulatory domains.
Rationale: To streamline compliance and reduce administrative burden while maximizing the value of DPP data.
Source: Ideas for reducing the reporting burden using the DPP (working_group)
Priority: Medium
Risk: Low: Depends on existing systems and data integration.
Verification Criterion: DPP data is reused in at least three different regulatory reports within 12 months.

ID: R-009
Statement: The organization shall ensure that all DPP data handling complies with GDPR and other relevant data protection regulations.
Rationale: To protect consumer and supplier data and avoid legal penalties or reputational damage.
Source: General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
Priority: High
Risk: Medium: Requires ongoing compliance monitoring.
Verification Criterion: Annual independent audit confirms GDPR compliance with DPP-related data processing.

ID: R-010
Statement: The organization shall monitor and report on the environmental and social impact of its product portfolio using DPP data to inform future sourcing and product development decisions.
Rationale: To support sustainable product innovation and market leadership in sustainability.
Source: Draft Preparatory Study on Textiles for Product Policy Instruments (working_group)
Priority: Medium
Risk: Low: Depends on internal decision-making processes.
Verification Criterion: Annual impact report is published and used in strategic planning meetings.
