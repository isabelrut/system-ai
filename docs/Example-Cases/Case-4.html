Digital Product Passport Requirements for a Large Retailer in the Toy Sector with Optimizing Digital Maturity and Competitive Advantage Aim

DPP-TOYS-001
Statement: When acting as a retailer of toys within the EU, the organization must ensure that all digital product passports (DPPs) linked to the products it sells contain accurate, up-to-date, and legally compliant data as defined by Regulation (EU) 2023/... (as per the Toy Safety Regulation proposal).
Rationale: To comply with EU legislation, retailers are responsible for verifying that all products they sell have valid and accessible DPPs to ensure product safety and traceability.
Organization Benefits: Reduces legal risk and supports brand trust through transparency.
Source: Proposal for the regulation on the safety of toys (Commission)
Priority: Must (M) – Legal non-compliance leads to penalties and reputational damage.
Risk of Implementation: Medium – Requires integration with supplier systems and may involve supplier audits.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: All DPPs linked to sold toys are verified for compliance via automated or manual audit systems.

DPP-TOYS-002
Statement: The organization must implement a system that enables real-time access to DPPs for all toys sold in its stores or online, including digital access for customers upon request.
Rationale: To align with consumer rights and EU transparency goals, customers must be able to access product safety and sustainability data.
Organization Benefits: Enhances customer trust and supports marketing through transparency.
Source: Proposal for the regulation on the safety of toys (Commission)
Priority: Must (M) – Legal requirement for product transparency.
Risk of Implementation: Medium – Integration with e-commerce platforms and in-store systems may be complex.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: Real-time access is provided to DPPs across all sales channels, with a 99% uptime SLA.

DPP-TOYS-003
Statement: The organization must establish a process to audit and verify the accuracy of DPP data supplied by upstream suppliers and manufacturers.
Rationale: Retailers are responsible for ensuring that data in DPPs is accurate, particularly for safety and compliance-related fields.
Organization Benefits: Reduces liability and improves supply chain accountability.
Source: Proposal for the regulation on the safety of toys (Commission)
Priority: Must (M) – Non-compliant data can result in product recalls and legal action.
Risk of Implementation: High – Requires supplier cooperation and may involve data validation tools.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: An annual audit confirms that at least 95% of DPP data is accurate and verified.

DPP-TOYS-004
Statement: The organization should integrate DPP data into its internal product lifecycle management systems to support decision-making on product sustainability and supply chain resilience.
Rationale: Leveraging DPP data can help identify risks, improve sourcing strategies, and support ESG goals.
Organization Benefits: Supports strategic decision-making and enhances sustainability reporting.
Source: Commission Communication on competitiveness (March 2023)
Priority: Should (S) – Strategic advantage, not a legal requirement.
Risk of Implementation: Medium – Integration with existing systems may require customization.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: DPP data is accessible in business intelligence tools for at least three use cases (e.g., sustainability reporting, risk monitoring, supplier evaluation).

DPP-TOYS-005
Statement: The organization should participate in industry-wide DPP data sharing protocols to improve interoperability and data exchange with regulators and other stakeholders.
Rationale: Interoperability supports cross-border compliance and facilitates regulatory reporting.
Organization Benefits: Increases operational efficiency and aligns with EU digital agenda goals.
Source: Commission Communication on competitiveness (March 2023)
Priority: Should (S) – Supports long-term competitiveness and digital transformation.
Risk of Implementation: Medium – Requires alignment with external partners and potential data governance adjustments.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: The organization is part of at least one recognized DPP data exchange platform.

DPP-TOYS-006
Statement: The organization could use DPP data to develop a proprietary product transparency platform to differentiate itself in the market and attract environmentally conscious consumers.
Rationale: Going beyond compliance allows the retailer to innovate and build brand loyalty.
Organization Benefits: Provides a unique selling point and supports marketing efforts.
Source: Commission Communication on competitiveness (March 2023)
Priority: Could (C) – Optional enhancement for competitive advantage.
Risk of Implementation: High – Requires significant investment in user interface design and marketing.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: Launch of a consumer-facing transparency platform with DPP data accessible in multiple formats.

DPP-TOYS-007
Statement: The organization could leverage DPP data to support internal analytics for demand forecasting, product returns analytics, and recall management.
Rationale: Advanced use of DPP data supports business intelligence and risk mitigation.
Organization Benefits: Improves inventory planning and reduces operational costs.
Source: Commission Communication on competitiveness (March 2023)
Priority: Could (C) – Strategic but not mandatory.
Risk of Implementation: Medium – Depends on existing data analytics capabilities.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: At least two internal use cases are developed using DPP data for analytics.

DPP-TOYS-008
Statement: The organization won't be required to publish DPP data publicly unless specifically mandated by EU or national legislation.
Rationale: While transparency is a goal, publication is not currently a legal requirement beyond consumer access.
Organization Benefits: Protects sensitive supply chain information from competitors.
Source: Proposal for the regulation on the safety of toys (Commission)
Priority: Won't (W) – Not a mandatory requirement at this time.
Risk of Implementation: Low – No action required.
Verification/Validation Success Criteria: No public publishing of DPP data is implemented unless legally required.

Sources

  • Proposal for the regulation on the safety of toys (Commission) from https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/system/files/2023-07/COM_2023_462_1_EN_ACT_part1_v4.pdf
  • Commission Communication on the long-term competitiveness of the EU (March 2023) from Official EU Publications



Each requirement consists of an ID, a statement (the actual requirement itself), a rationale (a compliance-oriented reason behind the requirement), organization benefits (such as improved efficiency), a source (which are either published EU documents or EU working group documents), a priority (based on the MoSCoW method, which uses must/should/could/won't), a risk of implementation (about the possible complications that might occur), and system validation success criteria (to verify whether you adhere to this requirement).

Note that this set of requirements has been generated using a Large Language Model. Although the used LLM has access to a relevant set of documents about the Digital Product Passport, it is still sensitive to hallucinations. Hence, it is recommended to only use these requirements as a starting point in your journey with the DPP. No rights can be derived from this LLM output.
